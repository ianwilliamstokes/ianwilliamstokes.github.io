Formula SAE Suspension Rocker

During my freshman year from September 2016 to June 2017, I was a memeber of the suspension sub-team on Northwestern's Formula SAE Team. I was given the project of designing the front and rear rockers for the competition car for that year. This is a simple project that familiarized me with the suspension system, CAD design, and CNC manufacturing. The rocker is a pivoting piece that connects the shock, ARB, and A-Arm. Its position can be seen in the first image on the right.

I designed the two rockers in Solidworks and ran basic FEA on the models. The model can be seen in the second image on the right. The FEA can be seen in the third image on the right and in the image below. I then desinged the rocker CAM and machined the rockers from Aluminium alloy 7075-T7351 using a CNC. Finally, I finsihed the mahcining on a manual mill. The rockers were then installed on the car, shown in the last image on the right. They all worked effectively for competition.

This was one of my first engineering projects and gave me my first expereince in CAD, FEA, CAM, and CNC machining. This ended my time with the team however as I went on to focus more on leadership oportunities and research.
