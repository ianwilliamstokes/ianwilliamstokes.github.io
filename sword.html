Injection Molded Toy Sword

In the Winter of 2019, I, on a team of three, was tasked with designing and manufacturing an injection molded toy based on a local fourth grader's drawing. We designed a three part, snap-fit Water Psychic Secret Sword CAD model in NX (Unigraphics), designed a two aluminum molds based on the parts, and developed the CAM process for machining the molds. We then machined the molds using a Haas Minimill CNC machine before injection molding 100 polypropylene models of the sword. Finally, we complete the project by conducting a series of tests on the individual parts and fully assembled toy to assess sizing accuracy using go no-go charts and quality assurance practices.

For the design of the sword, we had to adapt our manufacturing method to meet the design requirements, while remaining within the bounds of our shop's capabilities. Specifically, the blade of the sword was extremely long and thin, which made the mold very difficult to machine with a smallest tool size of 1/16". The hilt of the sword was divided into two halves with a snap fit connection, a requirement of the project. The general design was based on traditional "lobster" style swords from Scandinavia. The connection between the two halves was two 1/16" pins with a locational interference fit of H7p6.

This project earned me tangible skills in CAD and CAM development as well as metals CNC machining and plastics manufacturing.
