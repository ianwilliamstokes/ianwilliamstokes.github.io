Gear Reducer Box Design

The Fall quarter of 2018, from September to December, I took a tribology (the study of gears) and bearing design course. The final project required me to design a full gear reducer system complete with three shafts, each with gears and bearings. The prompt for the project is shown in the first image on the right. It specified the imput power and the reduction in power required at each shaft. The radius, teeth number, and locking mechanism of the gears were all determined using extensive calculations based on this prompt. Then, the gears were designed and assembled using NX. Next, the shapts were made to fit the gears with a locking mechanism and have a large enough diameter to withstand the input torque. One of the shafts is shown in the second image on the left. Finally, the bearings were designed as angle rolling bearings for the ends of each shaft. Assemblies were created for each of the three shafts, and drawings were made of these assemblies. One of these drawings is shown in the image below.

This project taught me valuable skills in gear and bearing design based on given values or collected data, something that can be very useful in drive train design and other geared mechanisms.
