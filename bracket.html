Design for Failure Bracket

I took a fnudamental GD&T and CAD course in the Spring of 2018 from April to June. In this course, I was taught CAD in NX and used it to design a bracket for the final project. The bracket had to be designed to specific dimensioning and had to fail under a load betwenn 2000 and 2500 Newtons. Using CAD modeling, dimensioning a tolerancing, limits and fits, GD&T, simple manufacturing, statics, aterial selection, design for deflection and stiffness, etc, on a team, I designed and manufactured the bracket to the specefications of the project. I chose a U channel cross-section for the beam in compression for greater compressive strength and a simple bar shape for the beam in tension. The intention was for the compression beam to fail via buckling.

We built the bracket from sheet metal 1018 steel that was provided because it has a higher strength to mass ratio compared with the aluminum options. The completed bracket before test is shown in the image below. Notice the holding piece on the left side of the bracket is used to connect the compression test device to our design and places the load exactly where intended. The bracket failed during test at around 2050 Newtons with our design intendidng to fail at 2250 Newtons, one of just five in the class to fail within the design constraint. The buckled compression beam can be seen in the image on the right. The discrepancy in the amount of applied load at failure can be explained by the unreliable and imperfect manufacturing method of sheet metal bending, which leaves gaps in the beams and unevenly strains the material before test.
